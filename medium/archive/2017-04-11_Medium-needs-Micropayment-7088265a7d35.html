Medium needs Micropayment

Content monetization is one of the biggest challenges currently facing online media. Most online media companies rely on ad-generated revenue, which often means there’s no room for quality editorial content. This results in a proliferation of “bite-size” sponsored content from online media companies such as Buzzfeed, Vice, Vox and Refinery29. Only a handful (literally) of these companies make over $100m annually. Most of their sales come from corporate sponsors such as Levi’s and Nike. Many other serious editorial content companies have mostly gone bankrupt. (Disclaimer: I’m an early investor of Refinery29.)

Medium.com set out a mission in 2012 to rethink a new economic model determining which contents are told, clicked on, and compensated. Earlier this year, Medium decided to pare down the company. It takes a visionary to say no to big corporate sponsors and Ev Williams is definitely one of them. He refused to turn Medium into just another ad-based media platform. Most recently Medium introduced a subscription model: readers can pay $5 a month for a membership.

This solves Medium’s short term problem of running the company, although it’s not yet clear is how authors are compensated.

Steve Jobs has solved this problem

This happened in the music industry during the transition to digital music. Apple opened the iTunes Music Store on April 28, 2003, with 200,000 songs. (Simultaneously, Apple released its third-generation iPod.) This was a brilliant way to solve three problems: consumption, distribution and micro-payment of digital music. After all, the credit card processors would benefit from an increased volume of transactions. But they may not be entirely pleased with an increased volume of transactions from Apple, given the way iTunes handles payments for 99-cent apps and $9.99 albums. Apple aggregates purchases made over several days into batches, reducing the per-transaction fees that it pays to Visa and Mastercard.

Medium has solved the problems of consumption and distribution. The Medium app offers a delightful user experience when creating, distributing, and consuming content. But there’s no easy way to rewards the content creators.

There’s no simple mechanism for micropayment for online digital content

Brave, a new browser startup headed by a former CEO of Mozilla, has tried to address this by building a new browser which has a built-in digital wallet and ad-block. It recently added the cryptocurrency token Ethereum as a way to reward content creators. It is definitely a brave new approach, but we have seen browser wars and know how the story ends. There is also an upcoming W3C web payment standards could be leveraged for micro payments as well, but these improvements are a long way in the future.

Don’t be Evil. Don’t be Greedy?

Google makes a lot of money through online ads. Google certainly can enable micropayment in their Chrome browser by adding digital wallets but they have a vested interested to keep ads floating around the Internet.

We need new tools and new approaches..

Dawn of Blockchain and Cryptocurrency era

Cosmos is a new public blockchain slated to be launched at the end of 2017. Cosmos just completed a historic fundraiser, in which they raised almost as much as Ethereum’s fundraiser did. It is poised to become one of the most promising public blockchains in 2017.

Cosmos is designed to facilitate micropayment transactions. It augments other blockchain networks such as Ethereum. Cosmos can commit blocks much faster than Ethereum’s proof-of-work algorithm.

The Cosmos-provided tool Basecoin allows anybody to create their own cryptocurrency token. Medium could design their own token — call it MDM — in order to reward content contributors. Contributors could then elect to exchange $MDM token to Bitcoin/Ether, and ultimately fiat currency.

Tapping 💚 would automatically transfer one MDM token from reader to the writer. There’s also donation buttons, if people want to contribute more money.

Perhaps this could be a medium-term solution for the broken ecosystem.

Open Invitation to Evan Williams

If Medium were to launch their own cryptocurrency and offer MDM token sales, there will be no doubt that MDM would become one of the top 10 cryptocurrencies overnight. Every writer could earn MDM directly from its readers without corporate sponsors standing between them. We can help with the entire process, from creating the token, to setting up Medium fundraiser, to helping launch Medium’s first public blockchain.
