Bug Bounty Program for Tendermint & Cosmos

At Tendermint, we believe that security researchers and white hat hackers are a vital part of building strong, resilient cryptocurrency protocols. To preemptively detect and fix bugs/edge cases while actively supporting the work that hackers and researchers do to find them, we are launching the Tendermint & Cosmos Bug Bounty Program.

Depending on the severity and criticality of an issue, researchers who report bugs and respect our Vulnerability Disclosure Policy may be eligible for rewards through our Bug Bounty Program on HackerOne. The program will be open for submissions in the next few weeks, depending upon HackerOne. We are making the official Bug Bounty Program announcement today to encourage folks to get started scouring our codebases.

Tendermint Security Page: https://tendermint.com/security

Bounty rewards are based on many factors, including impact, risk, likelihood of exploitation, and report quality. Rewards for bugs will be classified into these categories for payout:

  • Critical — $2,500 and up
  • High — $1000 and up
  • Medium — $500 and up
  • Low — $100 and up
  • Note — $50 and up

All program rewards will be paid in ETH or BTC, and will be calculated using prices at time of payment.

There is no maximum program reward, but we will reward creative or severe bugs accordingly. Tendermint will evaluate each report and will be responsible for rating the severity of each bug submitted. Depending on the severity of a bug and the quality of a report, we may choose to reward a lower-tier bug at a higher-tier level.

If we receive duplicate bug reports, we will award a bounty to the first person who reported the issue.

Once resolved, any valid vulnerabilities reported to this program will be disclosed publicly.

Program Scope

At present, the following Tendermint repositories are “In Scope” and thus eligible for the bounty (note some sub-packages and files are not in-scope):

github.com/tendermint/tmlibs

  • Example bugs include: lost writes or updates, race conditions, deadlocks, incorrect timers or use of time, inconsistencies between interface implementations, etc.
  • Not in scope: packages events, flowrate, logger

github.com/tendermint/go-wire

  • Example bugs include: inconsistent marshalling (ie Decode(Encode(x)) != x), allocate more memory than max specified, etc.
  • Not in scope: packages cmd, expr , nowriter

github.com/tendermint/go-crypto

  • Example bugs include: incorrect signatures, secret information leaks, timing attacks, etc.
  • Not in scope: Nothing!

github.com/tendermint/abci

  • Example bugs include: inconsistent marshalling, ie., Decode(Encode(x) != x), struct fields not copied when moving between protobuf and Go types, responses returned out of order, incorrect behaviour or lost data in example applications
  • Not in scope: all grpc_*.go files and GRPC related code, and the scripts package

github.com/tendermint/tendermint

  • Example bugs include: Denial of Service, validate invalid data, fail to sync despite good connectivity to honest peers, payloads that cause panic, lost data or messages, non-determinism in consensus data structures
  • Not in scope: rpc/grpc package

More

We encourage folks to look for bugs that may only appear when running a Tendermint network. Currently, the only application in scope for such a setup is the PersistentDummy , either running in-process with the — proxy_app=persistent_dummy or out-of-process via the socket server (ie. not GRPC). To be eligible for the bounty, the bug must be valid with less than 1/3 of the voting power being malicious in a network that satisfies a partial synchrony assumption. Of course, any violation of consensus safety with less than 1/3 of the voting power being malicious is eligible, regardless of the network assumptions.

In the coming weeks and months, we will be expanding the scope by adding in additional repositories. Stay tuned to find out when!

Scope Exclusions

We’ve designed our bug bounty program to focus on discovering bugs that have the biggest impact on our security and development. Thus, anything listed above under Not in scope is not in scope!

The following domains are also out of scope in our bug bounty program:

  • https://www.tendermint.com
  • https://blog.cosmos.network
  • https://tendermint.readthedocs.io

Though bugs in the services that we use are important to us, they are ineligible for program rewards. Any bugs that are found in services that we use (i.e. Mailchimp, Meetup, RiotChat) should be disclosed directly to those services.

Scanner-generated reports and “Advisory” or “informational” reports that do not include any Tendermint-specific testing or context are ineligible for rewards. Additionally, social engineering vulnerabilities are ineligible for reward as part of this program.

Contact Us

If you find a security issue, you can contact our team directly at security@tendermint.com, or report it to our bug bounty program on HackerOne.

To report a security vulnerability through an encrypted channel, please email us so that a member of our team can verify and exchange public keys with you.
