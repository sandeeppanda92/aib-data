Crypto 4 Your Thoughts Hackathon—Recap & Winners Announcement

Recap

Cosmos, along with Purse, was a Platinum sponsor of the Crypto 4 Your Thoughts hackathon, which ended last weekend. There were well over 200 attendees and we spotted some very bright, enthusiastic, and passionate builders of the open source ethos in the Bay Area leveraging blockchain protocols like Tendermint and Bitcoin via the Cosmos-SDK, LotionJS, and bcoin.

To get the scoop on what happened at C4YT, read Purse’s blog post.

A lot of new people learned about the Cosmos Network and how to use the Cosmos-SDK and LotionJS, and in the process, we learned a lot about what our actual users (namely developers) required from us. We heard you loud and clear, and the feedback provided gave us guidance about what to build for a much improved user experience for developing application-specific blockchain dApps using frameworks that we provide.

Opening Tech Talk by Jae Kwon

In part due to a focus of the hackathon being on UI, the final contestants of turned out only to be bcoin projects, a requirement which many of the teams building on Cosmos-SDK and LotionJS did not meet.

We saw a lot of interesting projects built with the Cosmos SDK and LotionJS over the weekend and invited teams who built with those to present at a private screening with the Cosmos team. It turned out that we loved all of the projects and made a last-minute decision to disburse ecosystem grants as out-of-band awards to not just give as a one-time prize, but instead fund, outstanding concepts.

Winning Cosmos-based Concepts

Reforest

The Reforest team created nice Node.js modules, and presented a demo with a donation system for planting trees and putting donation receipts as records in a Merkle tree. Fun.

Takeaways for LotionJS

“We were surprised by how quickly the participants grokked how to write Lotion apps,” remarked Judd Keppel, lead maintainer of LotionJS.

“My hackathon experience with Lotion is that it’s super easy to set up and start tinkering with. I would say that initial environment setup is often a huge uphill battle for any blockchain project. Lotion completely annihilated that part of the battle.” — Unnamed hackathon participant

This seemed to be the consensus among all the developers building on the Cosmos stack. Every single team used Lotion’s official cryptocurrency module, coins, signaling how high the demand is from crypto-projects to create their own cryptocurrencies. During the hackathon, we updated the docs to address some common questions. We improved the developer experience for anyone trying to build a cryptocurrency on Lotion by adding a simple wallet generation tool to the coins npm module.

The Pillar team contributed valuable ideas for future module design patterns for complex Lotion middleware. One member of their team, Lola Dam, even helped debug a state encoding issue in Lotion core and submitted a pull request to fix a bug in the way the Lotion light client works in Electron apps.

The key to designing user-friendly software is to minimize the empathic distance between creator and consumer, so CY4T was an extremely valuable opportunity for us to test our assumptions about how people would use Lotion. We deliberately designed the developer onboarding workflow to be easy, enjoyable, and to unfold predictably for new developers. We’re excited by the participants’ reaction to it, and even more excited to continue improving the developer experience, especially around the application deployment process.

Grant Details

The Interchain Foundation, the non-profit foundation supporting the development of the Cosmos Network, will be distributing an ecosystem grant to support the R&D efforts of the winning teams. The total grant will be $15k USD per team, with half funded upfront, and half granted upon completion of an MVP of a define deliverable, followed by a technical blog post.

If you missed out on this year’s C4YT hackathon, not to worry. We will resume hosting Cosmos-run HackAtoms again over the course of this year, post-mainnet launch.
