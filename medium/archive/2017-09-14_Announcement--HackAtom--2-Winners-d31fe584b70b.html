Announcement: HackAtom #2 Winners

We have our winners!

The judges have come to consensus. Today concludes our 6 week long global hackathon. The results are in:

First Place Winner — ETGate

GitHub repo here: https://github.com/mossid/etgate

We were quite impressed with ETGate. The Solidity code that verifies IAVL + trees is a significant contribution. Congratulations!

Second Place Winner — Chainmint

GitHub repo here: https://github.com/chainx-org/chainmint

Based on the Tendermint consensus engine, Chainmint is inherited from Chain’s UTXO and CVM system. Down the road, this can become a Cosmos Zone to support cross-blockchain functionality with Chain.

In short, Chainmint is Tendermint + UTXO + CVM.

Congrats on second place!

Honorary Mention — C-ABCI

GitHub repo here: https://github.com/chainx-org/c-abci

[Edit: September 21, 2017]

Third Place Winner — Clover

GitHub repo here: https://github.com/okwme/clovers-dapp

The Clover team built a decentralized application (dApp) on top of Ethermint, a Cosmos zone. Additionally, they deployed a version that works with Metamask. One of the “weirdest yet coolest niche projects,” according to Sunny Agrawal, one of our Cosmos researchers, commended.

[Edit: September 30, 2017]

Volunteer Awards

Lipeng Yue, who runs the ChainX community in China, is an active organizer for Cosmos meetups. In August, he ran meetups in three cities (Hangzhou, Beijing and Shenzhen) introducing Cosmos and HackAtom to the Chinese community. He also translates a lot of our English articles into Chinese! Big thanks to Lipeng, the recipient of our $2k volunteer award!
