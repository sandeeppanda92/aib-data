What is Voyager?

Cosmos Voyager is the GUI, or mission control center to the Cosmos Network. You’ll be able to view visual graphics of the network status in real time, validator status, incoming blocks, and transactions as they happen. You’ll be able to control your Atom balance by sending, receiving, staking, and making block rewards. Most importantly, you’ll be able to influence the future of the network by voting on proposals through the governance interface.

If you’re a developer interested in building on top of Cosmos, you’re encouraged to fork Voyager (an Electron and Vue-based app) to quickly and easily create the GUI for your own Cosmos Zone. Our codebase is entirely open-source and we’re working hard to provide comprehensive documentation and guidelines for downstream usage.

Voyager Update #1

Welcome to the first of a regular bi-monthly Voyager update! The Voyager team plans out a number of new features and bug fixes to be accomplished over two week sprints. These updates are intended to give you an executive summary of each iterative improvement in the UI.

Voyager Additions

  • We welcomed David “NodeGuy” Braun to the Voyager team. David previously worked at Monax with Tendermint CTO, Ethan Buchman.
  • The features of the Voyager MVP have been defined.
  • The primary users of the Voyager MVP, we’ve identified, are fundraiser contributors, dApp developers, and validators/delegators.
  • We were blocked by stability issues with the gaia-2 testnet, so we rebuilt the mock server so that developers and users can test Voyager without a testnet.
  • Deterministic builds are highest priority, so we decided to containerize Voyager with Docker.
  • We have a new (work in progress) logo for Voyager, shown in the banner at the top of the page.

Voyager Bug Fixes

  • We fixed an issue with sorting validators.

In Progress

  • We identified the importance of onboarding new users, so we’ve been working on an onboarding wizard for Voyager.

Conclusion

That’s all for this sprint. For those of you anxious about testing a new Voyager release, please be patient. We will have 0.5.0 releases out soon for Linux, macOS, and Windows. Follow our current progress on our GitHub page. Thanks for reading!
