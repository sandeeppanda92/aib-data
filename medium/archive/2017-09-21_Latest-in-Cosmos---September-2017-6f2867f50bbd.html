Oramint — Oracle Forked Tendermint

As a testament to Tendermint’s Bonded Proof-of-Stake technology, Oracle forked Tendermint as their base layer technology.

Oracle submitted a patent application of their consensus algorithm which borrowed from Tendermint software written in 2015. We were used as a demonstration for their technology and referenced 30 times in the document! Patent pending.

Summary — Jepsen Security Analysis

The full writeup can be found here: http://bit.ly/2j0KTsU

Industry Best Practice

We think that every decentralized network protocol should be put through a thorough stress test to simulate how it would hold up on a live network. Leading by example, we funded Jepsen.io, a distributed systems security research tool, to conduct the very first in-depth security analysis on a blockchain consensus system — Tendermint Core.

Jepsen.io did a terrific job summing up how the Tendermint protocol works in their analysis.

“Like Bitcoin and Ethereum, Tendermint is a blockchain system. However, where Bitcoin defines a currency, and Ethereum defines a virtual machine for computation, Tendermint deals in opaque transactional payloads.”
— Jepsen.io

Key Findings from the Analysis

  • Three durability issues uncovered. These issues were around Merkleeyes, goveldb, and Tendermint WAL. We confirmed all three of these issues and are developing patches.
  • Safety guaranteed. “Tendermint appears to satisfy its safety guarantees: transactions appear linearizable in the presence of simple and complex network partitions, clock skew, and synchronized crash-restart cycles. In addition, Tendermint appears to tolerate byzantine faults on less than 1/3 of the cluster, including duplicated validators with or without partitions, dynamic membership changes, and file truncation.” — Jepsen.io

Recap: Wanxiang’s 3rd Annual Global Blockchain Summit 2017 — Cosmos Made a Splash in Shanghai Last Week

We are very excited for the road ahead — about the burgeoning blockchain ecosystem in China, about Ethereum scaling, and particularly, about our part in making the future of blockchains interoperable as well as scalable.
